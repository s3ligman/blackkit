Ongoing Projects

BLACKKIT UNFOLDING:

As part of the project series BLACKKIT UNFOLDING a new archival box of international performance art will be opened every month with BORIS NIESLONY. Box by box the performance archive will be unfolded and provide insight into the versatile depths of the archive.

Tatsumi Orimoto


TATSUMI ORIMOTO (*1946) is a performance and communication artist. An encounter with the "Bread Man", his "Art-Mama", "Communication Art" or the "Big Shoes" project can be seen. A performance artist with great output, vitality and humorous irony.


Esther Ferrer


Esther Ferrer (*1937) is best known for her performance art since 1965, both as soloist and member of ZAJ (until 1996). She performed throughout Europe, Cuba, Mexico, USA, Japan, Palestine. In 1999 she represented Spain at the Venice Biennale.


Lee Wen


Lee Wen (*1957 - 2019) was a Singapore-based artist and active in artist-run initiatives as part of The Artists Village in Singapore or the international performance art group Black Market International.In 2005 he got the Cultural Medallion in Singapore, in 2016 he won the Joseph Balestier Award for the Freedom of Art. He was one of the most important performance artists in Asia. The Asia Art Archive started digitalizing his personal archive in the early 1980s.

About

The International Performance Art Archive THE BLACKKIT contains more than 3500 boxes, portfolios, folders and holders with documents and performance art relics. The archive stock is stored on 73 shelves with over 300 metres total length as well as in 28 large crates filled with relics. The visual documentation department consists of slides, negatives, vintages and hundreds of hours on film, some of which are of considerable historical value. The archive’s contents are currently being indexed and catalogued to create a digital data base in order to also ensure external research and work with the archive materials

History

The name BLACKKIT for the Performance Art Archive goes back to the project DAS KONZIL. In 1981, about 70 artists from Germany and other European countries met at the invitation of the artist’s houses in Stuttgart and Hamburg with the aim of closely living and working together.

During the KONZIL, the artists met every evening at a central table to reflect on what they had experienced and to ask fundamental questions about art and discussing fundamental positions of developments in the fields of interaction, intervention, communication and intermedial arts. There was voting, arguing, and discussion; the meetings were carefully documented.

The founding moment of the international performance archive was the decision to preserve all the materials, created during Das KONZIL, in two black office cabinets. In the course of the question of a form of documentation of the project, the idea of a transportable container - a drawer - arose, which was to be transported from event to event, from meeting to meeting. It should keep the information, ideas, documents, relics, tools and symbols energetically present.

Rather, it is a sculpture of a process of collecting that has been going on for 40 years today and a source of inspiration for the present.

The Archive underwent many transformations. The first one was a question of material: the heavy wooden wardrobe was replaced by light metal cabinets according to the collection’s constant growth. The next transformation became necessary around 1993, because the material was not just compiled from various meetings but also raised questions surpassing mere documentation. It evolved into archive structures, which require a different approach on structure and processing.

The name DIE SCHWARZEN LADE finally became BLACKKIT ('black kit/drawer'; lit. trans.: ='black load/charge'), The BLACKKIT was born.
